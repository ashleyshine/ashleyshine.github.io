Technical

  • Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship (5 / 5) — Write code that is easy to maintain and work with. Be thoughtful about your code.
  • Automate the Boring Stuff with Python: Practical Programming for Total Beginners (5 / 5) — The world is your oyster if you know some basic scripting. Practical and fun.
  • Introduction to Machine Learning with Python: A Guide for Data Scientists (4 / 5) — A decent intro to Scikit Learn that focuses more on getting you up-and-running, than the math behind ML.
  • Creating a Data-Driven Enterprise with DataOps (4 / 5) — Case studies on how big tech companies are building and maintaining services at scale.
  • The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win (4 / 5) — A story about How To Build Things.
  • The Mythical Man-Month: Essays on Software Engineering (3.5 / 5) — Various essays on software engineering and project management.

Finance & Economics

  • The Bogleheads’ Guide to Investing (5 / 5) — If I could recommend one book to everyone, this would probably be it. Take charge of your financial planning.
  • Charles Schwab’s Guide to Financial Independence: Simple Solutions for Busy People (5 / 5) — Somewhat dated but accessible and still has valuable lessons.
  • A Random Walk Down Wall Street (4 / 5) — Slightly less accessible than Bogleheads, but a good dive into what does and doesn’t work in investing.
  • Hard Landing: The Epic Contest for Power and Profits That Plunged the Airlines into Chaos
